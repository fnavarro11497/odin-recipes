CKO's Pizza

What & How:

New York pizza is my favorite style of pizza. Sure, I love me a neo-Neapolitan, sit-down-with-a-fork-and-knife pizza on occasion, and grilled pizzas are fantastic in the summer. Even chewy, Roman-style pizza bianca has its place. But the pizza I find myself most often craving is of the simple, by-the-slice, medium-thin, crusty, and lightly chewy style.

Ingredients

  • 22 1/2 ounces bread flour, plus more for dusting (638g; about 4 1/2 cups)
  • 0.5 ounce granulated sugar (15g; about 1 1/2 tablespoons)
  • 0.35 ounce kosher salt (10g; about 1 tablespoon)
  • 0.35 ounce instant yeast (10g; about 2 teaspoons)
  • 15 ounces lukewarm water (415g; about 1 3/4 cups)(see note)
  • 3 tablespoons (45ml) extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 batch New York Style Pizza Sauce
  • 1 pound grated full-fat dry mozzarella cheese (454g; about 4 cups), placed in freezer for at least 15 minutes

Steps:

  1. Combine flour, sugar, salt, and yeast in bowl of food processor
  2. Transfer dough ball to lightly floured surface and knead once or twice by hand until smooth ball is formed.
  3. At least two hours before baking, remove dough from refrigerator and shape into balls by gathering dough towards bottom and pinching shut.
  4. One hour before baking, adjust oven rack with pizza stone to middle position and preheat oven to 500°F (260°C).
  5. Spread approximately 2/3 cup sauce evenly over surface of crust, leaving 1/2- to 1-inch border along edge.
  6. Evenly spread 1/3 of cheese over sauce.
  7. Slide pizza onto baking stone and bake until cheese is melted with some browned spots and crust is golden brown and puffed, 12 to 15 minutes.
  8. Transfer to cutting board, slice, and serve immediately.
  9. Repeat with remaining two dough balls, remaining sauce, and remaining cheese.
